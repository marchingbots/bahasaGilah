VIQ team

THE MONITOR - people who are in the courtroom, ensure everything is being recorded properly.

THE TRANSCRIPTIONIST and THE EDITOR - they make an accurate written record of what's happening int he court room. They receive the audio file from court room.

The TRANSCRIPTIONIST produces transcript directly from that sound.

The EDITORS receive raw STT output generated by VIQ's speach to text engine and edit the output to make a polished transcript.
The TRANSCRIPTIONIST and the EDITORS work together to complete transcripts in timely manner.

TRANSCRIPT DELIVERY OFFICER - makes sure the broad formatting of the transcript is correct [conforms to client standards]. Merges all the short documents into one final document with coverpage, headers and footers. Their proof reading is cursory onceover.

Matter Artefact

Each court matter we produce has its own matter artefact (MA). This is a shared glossary that all transcriptionists and editors who work on a matter contribute to.

The link to it is here.

Each matter has its own matter artefact, which is found at the relevant jurisdiction’s Matter Artefact SharePoint site by searching for matter number (NetScribe jurisdictions)

The purpose of the MA is to ensure there are consistent spellings across transcripts completed by multiple people. Even when it’s not possible to confirm a spelling, a single unconfirmed version is added to the MA (followed by a (?)) to ensure consistency.
